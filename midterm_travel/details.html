Where to Eat in NYC

New York has just about every kind of food you could want. Whether you're looking for tacos, dumplings, fancy pasta, or the perfect bagel, there’s a spot for it. This list has two solid places for each cuisine and one bonus pick for American; all picked for being delicious and genuinely loved. (The red stars are michelin star restaurants).

  • Indian
  • Japanese
  • Italian
  • Mexican
  • Chinese
  • American
  • NYC Classics

Indian

Tamarind ★

Tamarind is polished and calm, with rich curries, soft naan, and flavorful appetizers. It's great for a nicer dinner without feeling too formal.

Dhamaka ★

Dhamaka is loud, exciting, and full of flavor. The dishes come from parts of India most menus skip, and the food here definitely brings heat.

Japanese

Ippudo

Ippudo serves deep, rich ramen in a fun, high-energy space. The pork buns are soft and perfect, and the ramen is one of the best in the city.

Sushi Nakazawa ★

Sushi Nakazawa is calm and focused, with each piece of sushi served one by one. It's simple, beautiful, and all about the details.

Italian

L’Artusi

L’Artusi has amazing pasta, great olive oil cake, and a fun, lively dining room. It’s one of those spots where everything just works.

Rezdôra ★

Rezdôra focuses on handmade pastas and dishes from Northern Italy. It’s cozy and the food feels personal, like something passed down.

Mexican

Los Tacos No.1

Quick, loud, and packed with flavor. The tacos are cooked fresh on the grill, and the adobada is especially popular for good reason.

Cosme ★

Cosme mixes bold Mexican flavors with a modern, creative feel. The duck carnitas and corn desserts are two things people talk about for days.

Chinese

Joe’s Shanghai

This spot is all about soup dumplings. The place moves fast, the menu is huge, and it always hits the spot when you're craving something warm.

Hutong ★

Hutong is sleek, moody, and full of flavor. Their spicy beef and dim sum plates are favorites, and the space itself is worth seeing.

American

J.G. Melon

Simple, solid, and always satisfying. J.G. Melon is known for its burger and no-frills diner energy. It’s been around forever for a reason.

Eleven Madison Park ★

This place goes big with a multi-course vegan tasting menu that still manages to feel fresh and thoughtful. It's one of NYC’s most famous restaurants.

Jack’s Wife Freda

This place feels like a cool neighborhood café. The shakshuka, grilled halloumi, and fresh juices make it perfect for a chill brunch with friends.

NYC Classics

Katz’s Deli

Pastrami on rye, soda in a glass bottle, and a counter full of yelling staff. Katz’s is loud, iconic, and a must-do for anyone visiting the city.

The Modern ★

Attached to the MoMA, The Modern is clean, artistic, and thoughtful. The food is plated with care and the flavors feel just right.
