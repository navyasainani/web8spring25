Iconic Places to Visit in New York City

The Statue of Liberty is a must-see in New York City. The ferry ride to Liberty Island also gives you amazing views of the iconic New York City skyline.

The Empire State Building has an incredible observation deck where you can see the whole city. Sunset is the best time to go.

The Metropolitan Museum of Art, also known as The Met, has thousands of works from around the world. It's one of the best places in the city for art lovers.

Central Park is a nice spot to walk around, relax, or have a picnic. Gossip Girl fans will recognize some filming spots, and shopping on Fifth Ave is just a few blocks away.

It's crowded and bright and loud, but Times Square is something you have to see at least once. You can shop, catch a street performer, or just take in the lights.

Broadway is where you'll find world-famous shows like Hamilton, Wicked, The Lion King, and Aladdin. If you can get tickets, it's totally worth it.

SoHo is known for its popular stores, art galleries, and cobblestone streets. It's a great place to shop, grab coffee at a cafe, and maybe even spot a celebrity or two.

The Hamptons are a popular weekend getaway from New York City. Take the Long Island Expressway and you'll find beaches, cute towns, and amazing ocean views. Yes, the traffic's real; but so are the sunsets.
